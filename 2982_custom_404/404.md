## The {admiral} Quiz

Did you know that the [admiral](https://pharmaverse.github.io/admiral/)
logo is inspired from the [Red
Admiral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanessa_atalanta), which is a
species of butterfly? Test your knowledge with this butterfly quiz
before flying away!

**Question 1: Yes, “Red Admiral” is a species of butterfly… but what is
its scientific name?**

1.  *Danaus plexippus*

2.  *Vanessa atalanta*

3.  *Papilio machaon*

4.  *Limenitis archippus*

**Question 2: Butterflies taste their food in a rather unusual way.
Which body part contains their taste receptors?**

1.  Antennae

2.  Proboscis (tongue)

3.  Tarsi (feet)

4.  Wing tips

**Question 3: Red Admirals belong to the family Nymphalidae. What is a
unique physical characteristic of this family that sets them apart from
others?**

1.  They have no antennae

2.  They appear to have only 4 legs instead of 6

3.  They are completely silent when flying

4.  They lack a proboscis

**Question 4: Most people know caterpillars eat leaves, but the Red
Admiral caterpillar is picky. What is its primary host plant?**

1.  Milkweed

2.  Stinging nettles

3.  Oak leaves

4.  Parsley

**Question 5: Which of the following is the largest butterfly species in
the world, with a wingspan of up to 11 inches (28 cm)?**

1.  Monarch

2.  Blue Morpho

3.  Queen Alexandra’s Birdwing

4.  Atlas moth

**Question 6: Technically, a butterfly pupa is called a “chrysalis.” How
does this differ from a “cocoon”?**

1.  A cocoon is made of silk; a chrysalis is a hard skin

2.  A cocoon is hard; a chrysalis is soft

3.  Butterflies make cocoons; moths make chrysalises

4.  They are exactly the same thing

**Question 7: Butterflies can see colors that humans cannot. Which part
of the light spectrum is visible to them, helping them find nectar
guides on flowers?**

1.  Infrared

2.  Ultraviolet

3.  X-ray

4.  Gamma ray

**Question 8: Butterflies and moths are often confused. What is the most
reliable way to tell them apart by looking at their antennae?**

1.  Butterfly antennae are always red

2.  Butterflies do not have antennae

3.  Moth antennae are clubbed at the ends

4.  Butterfly antennae have clubbed tips; moth antennae are feathery or
    tapering

## Quiz Answers

**Question 1:** B) *Vanessa atalanta*

**Question 2:** C) Tarsi (feet)

**Question 3:** B) They appear to have only 4 legs instead of 6

**Question 4:** B) Stinging nettles

**Question 5:** C) Queen Alexandra’s Birdwing

**Question 6:** A) A cocoon is made of silk; a chrysalis is a hard skin

**Question 7:** B) Ultraviolet

**Question 8:** D) Butterfly antennae have clubbed tips; moth antennae
are feathery or tapering
